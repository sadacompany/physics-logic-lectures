Simple Harmonic Motion

A mass attached to a spring oscillates back and forth. Explore how mass, spring constant, and amplitude affect the period and energy distribution. Watch sinusoidal motion emerge from a simple restoring force.

What's Happening

Press Play to release the mass from its initial displacement. The spring pulls it back toward equilibrium, but inertia carries it past, creating oscillation. The motion is perfectly sinusoidal: x(t) = A cos(wt).
