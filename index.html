Our Philosophy

Why Logic, Not Formulas?

Understanding a principle is worth infinitely more than memorizing a hundred equations. When you grasp the why, the formulas write themselves.

Most physics courses begin with equations and expect students to apply them mechanically. This approach crumbles the moment a problem deviates from a textbook template. Students pass exams but never truly understand what they have learned.

We take a fundamentally different path. Each lecture begins with a physical situation, a question, or a paradox. Through guided reasoning, you discover the underlying logic before a single equation appears. When the math finally arrives, it feels inevitable rather than arbitrary.

The result is knowledge that lasts, transfers across domains, and equips you to solve problems you have never seen before. That is the power of thinking like a physicist.

Learning Path

Your Roadmap

Four phases that build on each other, taking you from foundational thinking to the frontiers of modern physics.

Phase 1 — Lectures 1–2

Foundations

Build the thinking toolkit. Learn what physics is, how physicists reason, and master the language of units, dimensions, and measurement that underpins everything to come.

Phase 2 — Lectures 3–7

Classical Mechanics

Dive into motion, forces, energy, momentum, and gravitation. This is the heart of physics, where you will develop the core reasoning patterns used across every branch.

Phase 3 — Lectures 8–9

Waves and Thermal Physics

Explore oscillations, wave phenomena, and thermodynamics. See how energy concepts from mechanics extend into entirely new domains with elegant consistency.

Phase 4 — Lectures 10–12

Electromagnetism and Modern Physics

Unify electricity and magnetism, study optics, and glimpse the revolutionary ideas of relativity and quantum mechanics that reshaped our understanding of reality.
